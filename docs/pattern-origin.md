# Where the 9 Patterns Come From

The 9 patterns in The Archivist Method were identified through 19 years of behavioral observation in high-pressure social environments, cross-referenced with psychological research on habit loops, emotional regulation, and pattern recognition.

They are organized by activation intensity — from patterns that fire fast and hard, to patterns that are cognitive and sneaky, to patterns that are quiet and slow. This structure determines the order in which they are learned: you catch fire before you catch fog.

This is a working framework built for utility first. It was never designed to live in a research paper. It was designed to work at 11pm when you're about to blow up something you built.

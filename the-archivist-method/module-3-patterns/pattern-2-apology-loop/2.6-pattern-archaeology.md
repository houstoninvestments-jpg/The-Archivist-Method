## HOW THE APOLOGY LOOP INSTALLS

The Apology Loop installs when a child learns that having needs is dangerous. The installation requires at least one of these conditions:

**Condition A: Needs were punished.** The child asked for somethingâ€”attention, food, comfortâ€”and was met with anger, disappointment, or withdrawal. The lesson: asking costs more than not having.

**Condition B: A caregiver was volatile.** Unpredictable mood states. The child learned to monitor the emotional temperature and preemptively accommodate to avoid detonation. "Sorry" became a shield held up before the blow.

**Condition C: Parentification.** The child was responsible for the caregiver's emotional state. The child's needs were secondary. Or irrelevant. The role was: take care of them, not yourself. Needing something was selfish.

**Condition D: Conditional love.** Love was available when the child was good, quiet, helpful, invisible. Love was withdrawn when the child was loud, needy, difficult, or visible. The child learned: love has conditions. The primary condition is: do not need anything.

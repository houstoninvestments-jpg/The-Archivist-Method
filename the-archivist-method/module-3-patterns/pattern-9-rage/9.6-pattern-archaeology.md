- The loud person controls the room â†’ Be loud to be safe
- Flinching means you are the target â†’ Make others flinch so you are not
- Silence before explosion means danger â†’ Do not be silent; explode first
- Showing fear means showing weakness â†’ Convert all fear to anger

This logic kept Marcus safe in a household where the loudest person had the power. The rage was not dysfunction. It was a survival adaptation for a child who could not leave, could not fight back, and could not make his father stop. The one thing he could do was learn the program and run it himself.

But he is thirty-nine. His father has been dead for three years. And Marcus is running the program in a household where no one is a threat. His wife asks about a plumber. His daughter argues about a truck. His son spills milk. None of these are dangerous. All of them activate the program.

The program does not know the difference between his father's house and his house. It only knows: something disrupted the calm. Respond.

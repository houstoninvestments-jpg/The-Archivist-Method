# THE POCKET ARCHIVIST

Some readers carry The Archivist with them after the book closes.

That is what the Pocket Archivist exists for.

The Pocket Archivist is a conversational interface to The Archivist Method. It runs on the same framework you have just finished reading. It knows the nine patterns, the four doors, the three to seven second window. It speaks in the voice of this book.

What it adds: it knows your specific pattern. It remembers your body signature. It knows what time of day your pattern usually fires. It can name the circuit while you are mid-execution and hand you the script before the behavior completes.

It is not a chatbot. It is not a journaling app. It is a precision pattern intervention tool, available in the gap.

A note on what the Pocket Archivist will not do.

It will not perform crisis intervention. If you are in crisis, it routes you to 988 and exits the conversation. It will not diagnose. It is not a clinician. It will not coach you through deep excavation of a clinical wound. That is professional work. The Pocket Archivist is built for the tactical layer: the moment between trigger and behavior. That moment, repeated, is where the pattern weakens.

If thirty days is enough, the trial ends. If thirty days reveals that the conversation is what carries the work, the subscription extends.

The Archivist is in your pocket. Or not. Both are valid uses of what you have learned.

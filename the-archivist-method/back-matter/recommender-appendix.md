# FOR THE PERSON HANDING THIS BOOK TO SOMEONE ELSE

You are not the reader.

You are the person who saw the pattern in someone you care about. You read enough of the book to know it is the right book for them. Now you are at the question of how to give it to them without activating the very pattern you are trying to address.

This appendix is for you.

---

## WHAT TO SAY WHEN YOU HAND IT OVER

The most effective handoff is the shortest. "I read this. I thought of you." Then leave. No explanation. No expectation that they read it. No follow-up email.

If they ask what it is about, say: "It is about behavioral patterns. There are nine. The book identifies them and gives a method to interrupt them. I think one of them is yours. I will not say which one."

Then leave again.

The pattern will defend itself. The pattern is what they use to feel safe. Naming a pattern to a person who is running it is naming the floor they are standing on. They will resist. The resistance is data. It is not personal.

---

## WHAT THE BOOK WILL DO FOR THEM

It will give them accurate names for what they have been doing. It will explain why their previous attempts to stop have not worked. It will give them a framework for interrupting the pattern in the gap. If they read it and apply the protocol for ninety days, the pattern will weaken.

If they read it and put it on a shelf, nothing changes. This is the most likely outcome on first handoff. That is fine. Some people read the book they were given two years after they were given it. The book waits.

---

## WHAT THE BOOK WILL NOT DO FOR THEM

It will not make them ready to do the work. Readiness is the threshold the pattern itself decides whether to cross. You cannot cross it for them. No one can.

It will not replace clinical care if their pattern has produced clinical consequences. Substance use, violence, self-harm, suicidal ideation. These need a clinician. The book is a companion to clinical care, not a replacement for it.

It will not protect you from being on the receiving end of their pattern while they decide whether to do the work. Section 8.4 of the book is for you, on that.

---

## WHAT YOU CARRY

You did the part you can do. You saw clearly. You named accurately. You handed over a tool that contains a framework. The rest belongs to them.

Recognition is not control. Hand the book. Step back. Wait.

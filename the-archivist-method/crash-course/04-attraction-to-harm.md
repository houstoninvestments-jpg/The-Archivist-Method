## 2. MECHANISM

The cocktail. That is what your body is calling chemistry.

Three chemicals in a specific mix. Dopamine: the reward chemical. Norepinephrine: the alertness and arousal chemical. Cortisol: the stress chemical. The mix produces the heart racing, the appetite suppression, the obsessive thinking, the cannot-eat-cannot-sleep state that the culture calls falling in love. The same mix is what the body produces under threat. The cocktail does not know the difference. The cocktail just is.

The amygdala stores attachment templates in implicit memory before language develops. The template is not a thought. It is a neurochemical signature. The specific cocktail that flooded the body when love arrived in childhood is what the template encodes. If love arrived clean, the template encodes calm-and-warmth as the marker for love. If love arrived mixed with fear (an unpredictable caregiver, intermittent warmth, hot-and-cold attention) the template encodes fear-arousal as the marker for love. The template was written by a child who had no choice in the writing.

When you meet someone new as an adult, the limbic system runs a fast match against the template. The match happens below conscious awareness. If the new person produces the template's cocktail, the system fires recognition: this is love. The prefrontal cortex, which can assess actual safety, comes online seconds later and gets overruled. The body has already labeled the experience. The mind arrives to the labeling and writes the cover story.

Why this circuit fires for danger.

In the Original Room, the room where the pattern was installed, love arrived alongside unpredictability. The caregiver was warm sometimes and cold other times. Present sometimes and absent others. Loving in some moments and harsh in others. The child's nervous system did not get to separate the warm from the cold. They came in the same package, from the same person, in the same body. The body learned: love is intermittent. Love is unpredictable. Love is the high after the low. The cortisol-dopamine cycle of an inconsistent caregiver became the template for what love feels like.

The amygdala does not check whether the original room is the current room. It fires the same recognition in the new room as the old. A new person walks into your life. The amygdala scans them against the template. If they show signs of unpredictability, intensity, intermittent warmth, the template fires: match. The cocktail floods. You call this chemistry.

Intermittent reinforcement is the most durable training a nervous system can receive. It is the same schedule that builds gambling addictions. The reward is uncertain, and the uncertainty is what makes the pull so strong. The dopamine system does not learn from consistent love as efficiently as it learns from variable love. Each return from cold to warm is a fresh dopamine surge. Each surge deepens the bond. The cycle is the addiction. The person is not the addiction.

When you meet someone safe, the body runs the template match and finds nothing. No cocktail. No surge. The template says: this is not love. The body codes the absence as flatness. You experience the safe person as nothing because nothing is what your body has been trained to expect from genuine safety. The boredom you feel is not their fault. It is the absence of the cocktail.

The pattern math is always wrong. The automatic thoughts ("this one is different," "the chemistry is too strong to ignore," "I have never felt this way before") romanticize the cocktail and dismiss the safe person whose body never produced it. The math decides for you what is real and the answer is always: the intense one. The math is the cocktail thinking out loud.

## 3. BODY SIGNATURE

Two signals, not one. The fire and the flatness.

Most patterns have one body signature. This one has two, and they alternate by who is in the room. The fire activates around the dangerous person. The flatness activates around the safe person. Both are the pattern. The fire is the cocktail flooding. The flatness is the cocktail absent. The body cannot tell which signal it should trust because both are operating exactly as the template trained them to operate.

The first signal is the fire. Heart rate elevated. Pulse moving into the throat. Energy spiking to a level that would be alarming in any other context but feels exactly right here, the way the cocktail has always told the body love is supposed to feel. This is the primary marker around the dangerous person, and the most deceptive marker in the pattern, because the body has never produced this signature without coding it as desire.

The second signal is the butterflies. Stomach fluttering. Not the gentle warmth of connection. The sharp dropping sensation of a roller coaster. This is the fight-or-flight system activating in the gut, and the brain labeling the activation as attraction. Cortisol is rising. Norepinephrine is rising. The "butterflies" are the body bracing for impact while calling the bracing romance.

The third signal is the obsession. You cannot stop thinking about them. Replaying conversations. Analyzing texts. Building scenarios. This is hypervigilance relabeled as infatuation. Your brain is tracking what it perceives as a threat and calling the tracking love. Each replay deposits more dopamine. Each fantasy reinforces the bond.

The fourth signal is the cannot-eat-cannot-sleep state. Appetite suppressed. Sleep disrupted. These are autonomic markers of sustained sympathetic-nervous-system activation. The body is in a low-grade threat state, and the threat state is what your nervous system has trained you to call falling in love.

The fifth signal is the magnetic pull. A physical sensation of being drawn toward them. Hard to stay away. Hard to delete the message. Hard to walk past their door. This is the bond forming on the dopamine-prediction-error pathway. The body is locking on.

Then the second set of signals, around the safe person.

The sixth signal is the flatness. Nothing. No fire. No pulse-spike. No obsession. The body is silent. The pattern reads the silence as the absence of attraction. The silence is the absence of the cocktail. Not the same thing. Calm registers as empty in a body that has never been trained to recognize calm as connection.

The seventh signal is the restlessness. Around the safe person, you feel antsy. Bored. Like something is missing. What is missing is the cortisol. The body has been trained to require the cocktail to feel anything at all. Without it, it reads the room as understimulated. You will tell yourself there is no spark. You will be telling the truth about what your body is registering. You will be wrong about what it means.

You will not have all seven. You will probably have three or four around the dangerous person and one or two around the safe person. The order is your signature. Once you know it, the pattern stops being subtle. The pulse climbs around the wrong one, the body goes still around the right one, and you know what is about to happen before it happens. That gap is where the protocol lives.

Where I see mild drift: the longer prose in Section 5 ("The most common failure mode is making the request without the sorry, then watching the silence afterward, then panicking, then back-filling with the apology you skipped at the front") tilts slightly more analytical than the book's tighter operational sentences. Not generic self-help drift. Just slightly more verbose than the book's compression standard. Acceptable but flagged.

## 2. Recognition Test

The strongest Recognition paragraph in the crash course is the `[RECOGNITION]` callout closing Section 1: "You have apologized to people who did not need your sorry. They needed your question. They needed your opinion. They needed the actual thing you were asking for, asked plainly, so they could say yes or no. The sorry was not for them. The sorry was the pattern's price of admission, paid in advance, in case admission would otherwise be denied. The denial was not coming. It has rarely been coming, in adulthood, in this room, with this person. The bill keeps getting paid anyway." Honest judgment: it produces the *how does he know* response in the middle and weaker at the ends. The "price of admission paid in advance" image is fresh, not in the book, and earns the recognition. The closer "The bill keeps getting paid anyway" lands. The opening sentence is functional but not sharp; it sets up the image rather than producing recognition by itself. Architecture is specific (admission, denial, bill, payment in advance). Surface is open (no proper nouns, no platforms, no celebrity references, no dated artifacts). The reader supplies who they have apologized to. The comparison test against the book's 2.1 [RECOGNITION] callout ("'Sorry' is not your word. It is the pattern's word. It comes out before you choose it. Before you think it. You are not apologizing. You are flinching.") shows my version at 88 words against the book's 32. The book is denser, tighter, more compressed per word. The crash course version produces recognition but in a more ornamented register than the book's compression standard. It earns its length. It does not match the book's compression. Flagged.

## 3. Mechanism Integrity

The Mechanism section makes real biological claims and does not hedge. The dorsal vagal complex is named and is the correct anatomical structure for the fawn response per polyvagal framing. Larynx constriction and vocal fold tension under sympathetic-parasympathetic load are observable, measurable, and consistent with the literature on appeasement vocalizations in primates. The HPA axis is named and defined inline as "hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal, the body's stress response chain," which satisfies the load-bearing-term-defined-inline rule. Cortisol release from the adrenal cortex is correct mechanically. The insular cortex generating the felt sense of guilt before cognitive appraisal is consistent with the Damasio-tradition somatic marker work the book draws on. Broca's area looping back to prefrontal regions is a real pathway and is the correct mechanism for why spoken language engages the slow path. The "older than your prefrontal cortex by tens of millions of years" claim about the dorsal vagal system is a defensible approximation of the polyvagal evolutionary framing the book uses, though the specific number is loose; I would not defend it under cross-examination from a neuroscientist but it is not overstated for the lay audience. Zero therapy-speak. Zero "your nervous system is asking for safety" language. The Mechanism section holds.

## 5. Friction Protocol

Four of the five anti-symmetry rules are present in the file in load-bearing form. One is weaker than I claimed in the prior report. The rhythm break is real: "Notice what your body just did while reading that." stands as a one-line paragraph in the middle of Section 1, breaking the longer descriptive paragraphs around it, and "One script. Use it." stands as a two-sentence closer to Section 4. Both are deliberate, not accidental. The fragment / human-choice move is real: Section 4 opens with "One script. Not three." which is a fragment-feel pivot, and Section 7 contains "That is the crash course." as its own three-word paragraph and closes with "Keep collecting evidence." as its own line. The two Recognition-type callouts differ as required: `[FIELD OBSERVATION]` is 110 words in clinical third-person observing the subject's behavior, and `[RECOGNITION]` is 88 words in second-person on the price-of-admission frame; the angles, lengths, and textures genuinely differ rather than recapping each other. The recurring motif is real: "the throat" threads through Sections 2, 3, and 4 ("the throat went somewhere," "the throat closes," "Thinking does not reach the larynx"), and "the math" threads through Sections 1, 2, and 7 ("the math is always wrong," "the math is the pattern thinking out loud," "the math the pattern has been doing for you starts coming out the other way"). The book's 1.5 "Same trigger. Same body signature. Same automatic thought. Different action in the Gap" is a sharper example of the rule. The crash course performs the rule but does not nail it.
